# Clinical Reference — options assessed for her, evidence & doses

*Compiled June 2026 from research during her case discussion. All doses for
70 lb / ~32 kg unless noted.*

## 1. GI / ulcer management

| Drug | Dose (her) | Evidence/notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Omeprazole** (PPI) | 20 mg PO BID (≈1 mg/kg) before meals | Best-studied PPI in dogs; first choice for NSAID ulcer. Delayed-release; never crush. Taper off, don't stop abruptly |
| Famotidine (Pepcid) | 10–20 mg PO BID | Weaker than PPI; backup only; don't combine with omeprazole |
| **Sucralfate** | 1 g PO q8h on empty stomach (needs Rx) | Coats ulcer; excellent adjunct; separate from other oral meds by 2h |
| AVOID: omeprazole + sodium bicarbonate combos | — | 1,100 mg sodium/cap — bad for senior heart/kidneys |
| AVOID: Pepto-Bismol | — | Salicylate (aspirin family) — worsens bleeding |
| Pumpkin (100% canned) | 2–3 tbsp/meal | Soluble fiber for colitis/soft stool |
| Probiotic | Per label | Reasonable adjunct for colitis recovery |

## 2. Osteoarthritis — pharmacologic (vet-required, ranked for her)

| Option | What it is | Her fit |
|---|---|---|
| **Gabapentin** | Chronic-pain adjunct, ~10 mg/kg q8–12h typical start | GI-safe, dirt cheap generic. **Top priority script** for her |
| **Adequan (PSGAG)** | Injectable disease-modifying agent, series of 8 injections | GI-safe, good evidence. Excellent NSAID-free option |
| **Amantadine** | NMDA antagonist 3–5 mg/kg SID | Add-on for refractory pain; pairs with gabapentin |
| **Librela (bedinvetmab)** | Monthly anti-NGF monoclonal antibody | Strongest non-NSAID relief, GI-safe. ⚠️ FDA Dear-Vet letter (Dec 2024): neurologic & musculoskeletal adverse-event signals; risk/benefit discussion mandatory, monitor closely after dose 1 |
| Galliprant (grapiprant) | EP4 antagonist, GI-sparing-ish | Only after FULL gut healing + vet sign-off; prostaglandin pathway still involved — cautious given her bleed |
| Acetaminophen±codeine | Human analgesic | Vet-directed only, liver monitoring; NOT a home option |
| ❌ All classic NSAIDs | — | Permanently contraindicated for her |

| — | MSM | 500–1,000 mg/day | Usually bundled; don't double up |
| ⏸ | Turmeric/curcumin | HOLD | GI irritant + mild anticoagulant — wrong for her now |
| Skip | Plain vitamins E/C alone | — | Minor players; don't spend separately |

**Verdict on DM-01 (Dog is Human) multivitamin:** clean product, maintenance
doses only (~400 mg glucosamine, ~190 mg salmon oil at her 2-chew serving), no
chondroitin; ~$39–46/mo. Not effective for her arthritis or itch; poor value
for her priorities.

## 4. Itch / atopic dermatitis ladder

1. **Rule out cheap stuff first:** fleas (strict monthly prevention), secondary
   infection (chlorhexidine 4% baths 2×/wk — most common reason "Apoquel stopped
   working"), food trial (8–12 wk strict elimination diet = only valid test).
2. **OTC adjuncts:** fish oil (dose above); cetirizine 20 mg SID (plain only);
   diphenhydramine 50 mg q8–12h (sedating).
3. **Rx escalation (vet):**
   - **Cytopoint (lokivetmab)** — anti-IL-31 mAb injection q4–8wk; can COMBINE
     with Apoquel; very clean safety profile; best next step for her.
   - **Befrena (tirnovetmab)** — newer anti-IL-31 mAb (USDA-approved Dec 2025),
     6–8 wk dosing.
   - **Zenrelia (ilunocitinib)** — newer JAK inhibitor, higher remission rates
     than Apoquel but same class (SWITCH, don't stack); boxed warning.
   - Ciclosporin (Atopica); allergen-specific immunotherapy (ASIT) = only
     disease-modifying option.
4. **Senior red flag:** refractory/progressive skin disease at 15 → biopsy to
   rule out cutaneous (epitheliotropic) lymphoma.
5. **Never combine:** Apoquel + steroids, or two JAK inhibitors.

## 5. Peptides (gray-zone, owner has used before)

**BPC-157 (oral):** All evidence preclinical (rodent; ironically strongest for
NSAID-ulcer healing — her exact lesion). No canine trials, no regulated supply.
Gray-market practice ~200–500 mcg PO 1–2×/day for her size.
**KPV (oral):** rodent colitis data only; ~250–500 mcg/day.
**Position taken:** defensible as a SHORT (2–4 wk) adjunct during gut healing,
on top of omeprazole, from a COA-verified source, oral only. ⚠️ At 15,
BPC-157's pro-angiogenic signaling is a theoretical tumor-feeding concern
(occult neoplasia common in geriatric dogs) → avoid indefinite use. "It helped
before" ≠ proof — GI/itch wax and wane naturally.

**Overseas pharmacy imports of Rx drugs:** advised against — counterfeit risk,
no oversight; GoodRx + telehealth scripts are cheaper AND safer.

## 6. Low-cost care playbook

- **Telehealth vets** (Dutch, Vetster…): ~$30–60; can advise, often prescribe.
  Her shopping list for ONE visit: GI recheck, gabapentin, sucralfate, Apoquel
  refill, ask about Cytopoint pricing.
- **Vet school teaching hospitals:** typically 40–70% cheaper.
- **Human pharmacies + GoodRx:** omeprazole, famotidine, gabapentin, sucralfate
  are all cheap human generics on a vet script.
- **Aid charities** (apply to several simultaneously; RedRover & Paws 4 A Cure
  respond fastest): RedRover Relief, Paws 4 A Cure, Brown Dog Foundation,
  The Pet Fund, Frankie's Friends; Best Friends maintains a 100+ program list.
- **Payment tools:** CareCredit, Scratchpay; many clinics negotiate if asked
  directly.
