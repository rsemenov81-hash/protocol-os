## 4. Suggestions Made During the Discussion (by problem)

### 4a. GI bleed / colitis
- **Stop all NSAIDs permanently for this dog**; never substitute another NSAID.
- **❌ Never give human NSAIDs/analgesics:** ibuprofen, naproxen, aspirin, Tylenol (acetaminophen), or Pepto-Bismol (salicylate) — all can worsen the bleed/cause toxicity.
- **Omeprazole** chosen over lansoprazole (best canine evidence) and over omeprazole+sodium-bicarbonate combo (avoid the extra sodium load in a senior). Dose ~1 mg/kg, practically 20 mg PO BID.
- Optional/cheaper alt: **famotidine (Pepcid AC)** if omeprazole unavailable (but PPI preferred for an active bleed; don't use both together).
- Suggested asking a low-cost source about **sucralfate** (coats ulcer; cheap).
- **Bland diet, small frequent meals, hydration; + pumpkin** for the colitis component.
- **Red-flag / emergency triggers** (would override the no-vet plan): pale/white gums, weakness/collapse, ongoing black tarry stool, repeated vomiting despite omeprazole, refusing food >24–48h, distended painful abdomen, or fresh blood/jelly-like bloody mucus.

### 4b. Joint pain (NSAID-intolerant options)
- **Non-GI pharmacologic options to discuss with a vet:**
  - **Librela (bedinvetmab)** — monthly anti-NGF monoclonal antibody; GI/kidney-sparing; **BUT** presented with explicit safety caveats: FDA "Dear Veterinarian Letter" (Dec 2024) and 2025 reports of **neurologic and musculoskeletal adverse events** — a real risk/benefit conversation, especially at 15.
  - **Adequan (polysulfated glycosaminoglycan)** — injectable, GI-safe, disease-modifying; strong fit.
  - **Gabapentin** — GI-safe adjunct analgesic (the cheapest legitimate pain option once NSAIDs are out).
  - **Amantadine** — NMDA antagonist, add-on for refractory OA pain.
  - **Galliprant (grapiprant)** — EP4 antagonist, more GI-sparing than NSAIDs, but **only after full gut healing** and with caution given bleed history.
  - **Acetaminophen ± codeine** — vet-directed only; liver caution. (Not a home option.)
- **Supplements (best-evidence first):** Omega-3 fish oil **~1,600–2,400 mg EPA+DHA/day** (#1); glucosamine+chondroitin±MSM (Cosequin DS / Dasuquin, large-dog dose); **green-lipped mussel** (gentlest on gut); **UC-II undenatured collagen 40 mg/day**. **Hold turmeric/curcumin** (GI irritation + blood-thinning) until fully healed.
- **Non-drug pillars (highest value, free):** **weight management** (single most effective OA intervention), traction rugs, orthopedic bedding, ramps, short frequent walks, warm compress, rehab/laser/acupuncture/hydrotherapy if affordable.

### 4c. Itch / atopic dermatitis (Apoquel-partial)
- **Investigate *why* Apoquel is incomplete before escalating:** check for **secondary skin infection** (staph/Malassezia — #1 reason Apoquel "fails"), under-dosing, fleas, food-allergy component.
- 🚩 **Senior red flag:** new/refractory itch in a 15-yo can rarely be **cutaneous (epitheliotropic) lymphoma** — biopsy if lesions are refractory.
- **Escalation options (vet-directed):**
  - **Cytopoint (lokivetmab)** — anti–IL-31 monoclonal antibody; **GI-safe, very clean safety profile, can be combined with Apoquel** — flagged as the **best-fit next step**.
  - **Befrena (tirnovetmab)** — newer anti–IL-31 mAb (USDA approved Dec 2025, 6–8 wk dosing).
  - **Zenrelia (ilunocitinib)** — newer once-daily JAK inhibitor; higher remission than Apoquel but **same class (switch, not stack)**; boxed warning re: immunosuppression.
  - **Ciclosporin (Atopica)**, **allergen-specific immunotherapy (ASIT)** — disease-modifying, long-term.
- **At-home/OTC for itch:** properly dosed fish oil; **cetirizine (plain Zyrtec) 20 mg once daily** (never Zyrtec-D) or diphenhydramine; **chlorhexidine 4% shampoo/wipes** (best no-Rx tool against secondary infection); strict monthly flea prevention; oatmeal baths/cool rinses.

### 4d. Product assessed — "DM-01 Daily Multivitamin" (Dog is Human)
- Owner asked about this product (label reviewed from photos).
- **Verdict:** Clean, high-quality, human-grade **maintenance multivitamin** — but **functional ingredients are at token/maintenance, not therapeutic, doses** (glucosamine ~400 mg/day at 2 chews; salmon oil ~190 mg/day ≈ negligible omega-3; no chondroitin).
- **Effectiveness:** ~5/10 as a general multivitamin; ~2/10 for a geriatric dog's real needs (joints/omega-3/cognition). **Will NOT help itch or arthritis meaningfully.**
- **Price:** ~$39/mo subscription (~$46 one-time), 60 chews — premium, poor value for this life stage.
- **Recommendation:** Not worth the spend for her; money better spent on dedicated omega-3 + real joint support + a low-cost vet contact.

### 4e. Low-cost / no-vet access strategy (owner is budget-constrained)
- **Cheapest legitimate path:** veterinary **teaching hospitals** (40–70% less), **telehealth vet visits** ($30–60; can advise/sometimes prescribe), **GoodRx/Costco/human pharmacy** for generic pet meds (omeprazole, famotidine, gabapentin, sucralfate are cheap human generics).
- **Financial-aid charities** (apply to several at once): RedRover Relief, Paws 4 A Cure (fastest), Brown Dog Foundation, The Pet Fund, Frankie's Friends; Best Friends' 100+ program list.
- **Single highest-value spend identified:** one **~$30–60 telehealth visit** to (a) confirm the GI bleed is resolving, (b) refill Apoquel, and (c) unlock **sucralfate + gabapentin** — the three most useful items not available OTC.

## 6. Current At-Home Protocol (consolidated)

**Phase 1 — Heal the gut (now):**
- Omeprazole 20 mg PO BID before meals (whole), 2–4 wks then taper.
- Bland diet, 4–6 small meals; pumpkin 2–3 tbsp/meal; ensure hydration.
- No NSAIDs/human analgesics. Hold fish oil & turmeric until stool normal ~5–7 days.
- Apoquel continued unless actively vomiting.
- Daily log: gum color, stool (color/blood), appetite, vomiting, energy.

**Phase 2 — Joints (once gut stable):**
- Weight management (priority); comfort setup (rugs, orthopedic bed, ramps, short frequent walks, warm compress).
- Introduce green-lipped mussel + glucosamine/chondroitin chew; then ramp fish oil to ~2,000 mg EPA+DHA/day; consider UC-II 40 mg/day. One new item at a time, few days apart.

**Phase 3 — Itch:**
- Fish oil (dual benefit), cetirizine 20 mg once daily, chlorhexidine baths 2×/week + wipes, strict flea prevention.

**Throughout:** apply to charities now; budget for one telehealth visit to unlock sucralfate, gabapentin, Apoquel refill, and a GI re-check.
